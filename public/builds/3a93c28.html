Highlights

Unifies and refactors Trio's alerting pipeline: introduces a single TrioAlertManager that routes LoopKit device and glucose alerts, adds a configurable Glucose Alarms list (including new "Carbs Required"), tiered device/pump alarm severity with per-tier snooze and shared Day/Night window, catalog-based pump alert classification, unified in-app banner + UserNotification scheduling and centralized snooze handling. Moves the global Snooze UI from a hidden long-press into Settings → Notifications (canonical 15/60/180/360‑minute durations), deletes the old Snooze module and legacy SwiftMessages pipeline, and migrates callers to TrioAlertManager. Adds bundled CAF tones and a CriticalAlertAudioPlayer fallback, includes the time-sensitive UN entitlement plumbing, and replaces the Not‑Looping monitor with a re-armed delayed LoopKit alert. Accompanying unit tests, settings/UI changes, and fixes to snooze UI gesture handling were merged.

Alerts are now routed through a unified alert manager and Settings gains new sections (Glucose Alarms; Device/Pump & CGM Alarms; Day & Night Window). Glucose alarms become a configurable list (with per-alarm thresholds, schedule, sound and snooze) and a new "Carbs Required" alarm type is added. Device/pump alerts are configured by severity tier with per-tier sound/Override‑Silence behavior. The global Snooze All control is visible in Settings → Notifications (replaces the hidden long-press), using 15/60/180/360‑minute options. If a CGM app owns glucose alerts, Trio surfaces those under “Handled by CGM App” and suppresses duplicate alerts. In-app banner vs background UN behavior is unified; snooze actions are centralized so quick-snooze actions behave consistently. The Not‑Looping alert now uses a delayed re-arm and legacy SwiftMessages alerting and its dependency were removed. Minor test and UI fixes for snooze long-press behavior were included.
