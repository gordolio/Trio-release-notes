## Highlights

- **Unified alert system with customizable glucose alarms**
  - New customizable glucose alarms include urgent‑low, low, high, forecasted low, and Carbs Required.
  - Each alarm can use its own threshold, schedule, sound, and snooze settings.
  - A shared Day/Night window controls when alarms are active and the Add‑Alarm picker prevents overlaps.
  - Trio shows an in‑app banner when foregrounded and sends iOS notifications when backgrounded, with unified snooze.
  - Alerts are organized by severity and Trio hides duplicates when a CGM app already provides the same alert.
  - [View source](https://github.com/nightscout/Trio/pull/1203)
- **Move Snooze All to Settings and unify alerts**
  - Snooze All is now a button in Settings → Notifications instead of a hidden long-press on the glucose bobble.
  - Tapping Snooze All opens a sheet offering 15 min, 1 hr, 3 hrs, and 6 hrs snooze durations.
  - All in-app alerts now route through a single TrioAlertManager so alerts use a unified presentation.
  - Specific warnings (Garmin Connect missing, CGM open failures, stale calibration) now appear via the unified alert UI.
  - Info-level logger messages no longer surface as in-app banners and are log-only.
  - [View source](https://github.com/nightscout/Trio/pull/1243)
- **Fix snooze long-press behavior**
  - Long-pressing the CGM area now reliably opens the Snooze sheet.
  - Tapping the CGM area still opens CGM selection or the setup sheet as before.
  - [View source](https://github.com/nightscout/Trio/pull/1269)
- **Quick‑Pick Boluses via long‑press +**
  - Long‑press the + button on the home screen to open a Quick‑Pick Boluses sheet.
  - The app suggests up to five usual bolus amounts learned from your past 90 days and time‑of‑day patterns.
  - Tap a suggestion, then confirm and authorize (Face/Touch ID) to enact the bolus.
  - A new Treatments setting lets you enable or disable the Quick‑Pick Boluses feature.
  - If no suitable bolus history exists, the app shows a clear message explaining why no suggestions are available.
  - [View source](https://github.com/nightscout/Trio/pull/1248)
- **Default to Swift Oref; add 'Use JavaScript Oref' toggle**
  - Trio now uses the Swift Oref algorithm by default.
  - The advanced setting was renamed to "Use JavaScript Oref" and is off by default.
  - Turning on that setting switches Trio back to the legacy JavaScript algorithm.
  - Settings text and loop-status labels were updated to reflect Swift as the default.
  - [View source](https://github.com/nightscout/Trio/pull/1273)
